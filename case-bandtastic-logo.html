Bandtastic
- LOGO REDESIGN -
Problem

The problem is, that original logo hurts people's feelings the more they look at it. I JEST! The original logo was still created through mind maps & sketches, but it was during my first project, & I was following the design process in a strictly linear fashion. I was also brand new to using Sketch, & unaware of the details of vector editing. It was a placeholder solution to deal with the visual problem that Balsamic's style of wireframes created during the Information Architecture part of the process.

Solution

The solution became a well balanced logo that used an element of the original. It was simplified enough to be used & recognized in multiple brand color combinations.

Process
Centerpiece

I needed a centerpiece of the logo redesign. I wanted to really simplify to logo so it would be able to be used at different sizes, yet retain something from the original logo. The simplification was the choice to do a logotype, & the centerpiece was the b flat.

Choosing a Typeface

I wanted something that had characteristics of calligraphy like the b flat, but without being as complicated as a script typeface. I wanted it to have sharper edges like a flat marker or paintbrush, but without being too serif heavy. Serif refers to the tiny little toes that are on the edges of letters like on a typewriter, & the commonly used Times New Roman.

The first cuts came from getting rid of the more rounded edges, & where there was too low a stroke contrast. Stroke contrast refers to how the lines vary between thick & thin.

Next, I removed typefaces that were overall too thin, & again, that had too low a stroke contrast.

Now, it was getting difficult to eliminate choices. Aside from the top 3, the rest looked extremely similar. They all had a moderate stroke contrast, & all had the same tiny “shelf” on the top corner of each vertical line that had an angle on top. They all however, resembled the third from top font, while being slightly less bold, so were all eliminated.

Final Elimination Round

For the final elimination round, I wanted to see how close the typeface was to the b flat, just as an anchor to drive my decision. Right away I could see that the top option, Aclonica, was too round, even though I enjoyed the n dipping below the baseline.

I kept Amarante the center option this whole time, because of the C at the end, even though it had small serifs. That C at the end resembled the exact curve of the flat notation, but overall the font had too much of a curve on the straight lines. The option in the last row, Bangla MN, matched up with the curves of the musical flat the most, & was closest in overall character thickness.

Finished?

Overall Bangla MN matched up well with the musical flat, but I still felt unsatisfied.

Potpourri Collage

I tried mixing the favorite parts of my 3 finalists together just to see how it worked out.It was fun but, a bit all over the place.

Balanced

I brought it back a little & got some symmetry by placing only the c from Amarante at the end of Bangla & was quite pleased with the results. I thought it looked a bit cleaner with only the flat in front of Bangla MN, but the character & symmetry that the C from Amarante added was too much to resist! I nudged the c down just a couple pixels to dip it below the baseline like the musical flat.

Flat Height

What stood out to me is how tall the flat was compared to the rest of the typeface. I figured I could create an angle off of the other letters to work with & cut it down just a bit.

Final Details

The last couple of details I needed to catch, were to match the small slanted angles from Bangla MN, & to thicken up the vertical line on the musical flat to match everything else. FIN!
