Process
Step 1: Centerpiece

Insert musical flat. Insert musical flat. Insert musical flat. Insert musical flat. Insert musical flat. Insert musical flat. Insert musical flat. Insert musical flat.

I needed a centerpiece of the logo redesign. I wanted to really simplify to logo so it would be able to be used at different sizes, yet retain something from the original logo. The simplification was the choice to do a logotype, & the centerpiece was the b flat.

Step 2: Choosing a typeface

Choose sub headings consistent with rest of case study for these 4 rounds....ROUND 1!

Pic of round 1

I wanted something that had characteristics of calligraphy like the b flat, but without being as complicated as a script typeface. I wanted it to have sharper edges like a flat marker or paintbrush, but without being too serif heavy. Serif refers to the tiny little toes that are on the edges of letters like on a typewriter, & the commonly used Times New Roman.

ROUND 2

Pic of round 2

The first cuts came from getting rid of the more rounded edges, & where there was too low a stroke contrast in stroke thickness. That stroke contrast refers to how the lines vary between thick & thin.

ROUND 3

Pic of round 3

Next, I removed typefaces that were overall too thin, & again, that had too low a stroke contrast.

ROUND 4

Pic of round 4

Now, it was getting difficult to eliminate choices. Aside from the top 3, the rest looked extremely similar. They all had a moderate stroke contrast, & all had the same tiny “shelf” on the top corner of each verticle line that had an angle on top. They all however, resembled the third from top font, while being slightly less bold, so were all eliminated.

Final Elimination Round

For the final elimination round, I wanted to see how close the typeface was to the b flat, just as an anchor to drive my decision.

Right away I could see that the top option, Aclonica, was too round, even though I enjoyed the n dipping below the baseline.

I kept Amarante the center option this whole time because of the C at the end, even though it had small serifs. That C at the end resembled the exact curve of the flat notation, but overall the font was just not what I wanted.

The option in the last row, Bangla MN, matched up with the curves of the musical flat the most, & was closest in overall character thickness.

Fun balanced final

I brought it back a little & got some symmetry by placing only the c from Amarante at the end of Bangla & was quite pleased with the results. I thought it looked a bit cleaner with only the flat in front of Bangla MN, but the character & symmetry that the C from Amarante added was too much to resist! I nudged the c down just a couple pixels to line the top of it up with the bottom of the cross on the T, & to dip it below the baseline like the musical flat.
